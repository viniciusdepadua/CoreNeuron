13 /* interface to Random123 */
14 /* http://www.thesalmons.org/john/random123/papers/random123sc11.pdf */
15 
16 /*
17 The 4x32 generators utilize a uint32x4 counter and uint32x4 key to transform
18 into an almost cryptographic quality uint32x4 random result.
19 There are many possibilites for balancing the sharing of the internal
20 state instances while reserving a uint32 counter for the stream sequence
21 and reserving other portions of the counter vector for stream identifiers
22 and global index used by all streams.
23 
24 We currently provide a single instance by default in which the policy is
25 to use the 0th counter uint32 as the stream sequence, words 2 and 3 as the
26 stream identifier, and word 0 of the key as the global index. Unused words
27 are constant uint32 0.
28 
29 It is also possible to use Random123 directly without reference to this
30 interface. See Random123-1.02/docs/html/index.html
31 of the full distribution available from
32 http://www.deshawresearch.com/resources_random123.html
33 */
